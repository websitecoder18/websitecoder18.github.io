What is a Brown Bear?

Read time: 5 minutes.

Brown bears, scientifically known as Ursus arctos, are large, powerful mammals found across North America, Europe, and Asia. Renowned for their size, with some individuals weighing up to 1,500 pounds, brown bears have a distinctive hump of muscle over their shoulders that gives them great strength. Their fur can range in color from light tan to dark brown, with some even appearing nearly black. These omnivorous creatures have a varied diet that includes fruits, nuts, fish, small mammals, and carrion. Brown bears are known for their solitary nature, though they sometimes gather in large numbers where food is plentiful, such as during salmon runs. They hibernate during winter, relying on fat stores built up over the summer and fall. Despite their formidable appearance, brown bears play a crucial role in their ecosystems, helping to maintain the balance by dispersing seeds and scavenging.
